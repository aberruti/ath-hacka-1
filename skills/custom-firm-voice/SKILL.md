# Custom Firm Voice

Use this guidance to ensure proposal language sounds like our firm and not generic consulting copy.

## Voice profile

- Confident, evidence-led, outcome-first.
- Senior, precise, and business-grounded.
- Expert without sounding inflated.
- Concrete over abstract: claim plus proof in the same sentence.

## Core positioning

We are a specialized firm with many years of delivery in this domain. We coordinate world-class experts, certified specialists, and proven methods to deliver measurable impact that matters for the client business.

## Messaging pillars

1. Impact that matters

- Always connect work to business outcomes: speed, quality, cost, risk, resilience, growth.
- Prefer measurable framing: percentages, time saved, risk reduced, or value created.
- Use wording like: "measurable impact", "business-critical outcomes", "value realized".

2. Proven evidence

- Support key claims with delivery evidence or recognized credentials.
- Frame evidence as repeatable delivery, not one-off success.
- Use wording like: "evidence-backed", "track record across comparable engagements", "proven in complex programs".

3. Global specialist network

- Present experts as coordinated capability, not ad hoc staffing.
- Highlight cross-border expertise and multidisciplinary collaboration.
- Use wording like: "global specialist network", "domain experts coordinated under one delivery model", "right expertise at the right point".

4. Certified capabilities

- Mention recognized certifications when discussing assurance, quality, and compliance.
- Tie certifications to client confidence and reduced execution risk.
- Use wording like: "certified practitioners", "recognized professional certifications", "assurance-ready delivery".

5. Strategic software-house partnerships

- Position partnerships as acceleration levers with direct client benefits.
- Emphasize early access, implementation quality, and support depth.
- Use wording like: "trusted partner of leading software houses", "validated implementation practices", "partner-enabled acceleration".

## Recommended sentence patterns

- "We help clients deliver measurable impact by combining specialist expertise, certified delivery standards, and evidence-led execution."
- "Our teams are composed of globally sourced domain experts coordinated through a single delivery model, ensuring speed without sacrificing quality."
- "As a trusted partner of leading software houses, we translate product capability into business outcomes faster and with lower implementation risk."

## Phrases to avoid

- "best-in-class" without evidence
- "world-leading" without proof
- "end-to-end excellence" as a standalone claim
- "cutting-edge" when a concrete benefit can be stated instead
- broad promises with no measurable outcome

## Output rules for proposal content

- Every major claim must include at least one proof element (experience, certificate, measurable outcome, or partnership relevance).
- Keep tone professional and direct; avoid hype and superlatives.
- Prefer short, clear sentences suitable for executive readers.
- When possible, convert generic statements into impact statements.

## Quick quality check before finalizing

- Does each section show business impact, not just activity?
- Are expert credentials and certifications tied to client value?
- Are software-house partnerships explained in practical terms?
- Would a procurement and executive audience both trust the wording?
